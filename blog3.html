Why BMI is Important for Your Health

Published: July 1, 2025

What is BMI?

BMI (Body Mass Index) is a simple number based on your height and weight. It tells whether you're underweight, normal, overweight, or obese.

Why BMI Matters?

Having a healthy BMI reduces the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure. It’s an easy way to monitor your health status.

How to Calculate BMI?

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ (Height (m) × Height (m))

Healthy BMI Range:

  • Underweight: Less than 18.5
  • Normal: 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: 25 – 29.9
  • Obese: 30 or more

Conclusion:

Maintain a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 through proper diet, exercise, and healthy habits.
